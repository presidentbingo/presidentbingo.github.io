    Explain both development cycle and stretch goal

    Your Answer:

    A development cycle is the 'cycle' that the program goes throughout whilst being constructed. Typically, the cycle is done iteratively, meaning it is finished with a cycle of steps and increments as the program is being developed. First, the design document is made, then while following the design document, the development cycle is circulated as the programmer follows the design document and testing the program in parts step by step to get to the final product most efficiently. A stretch goal is a goal that is reached only if there is enough resources or time to do so after finishing the original plan made from the design document.
